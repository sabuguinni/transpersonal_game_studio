# ATMOSPHERIC LIGHTING CONCEPT DOCUMENTATION
## Transpersonal Game Studio - Environment Artist #6

### CRETACEOUS FOREST ATMOSPHERE VISION

**GOLDEN HOUR LIGHTING SYSTEM**
The prehistoric forest comes alive during the golden hour, when warm sunlight filters through the canopy of ancient ferns, cycads, and primitive conifers. This lighting creates a sense of primordial wonder and establishes the temporal setting through atmospheric cues.

**Key Visual Elements:**
- Volumetric fog rays piercing through dense vegetation
- Dust particles and pollen floating in warm sunbeams
- Atmospheric perspective creating depth in the forest
- Moss-covered fallen logs casting dramatic shadows
- Weathered boulders with lichen growth catching light
- Dappled light patterns on the forest floor

**Technical Implementation:**
- Directional Light positioned at 15-degree angle for golden hour
- Exponential Height Fog with warm orange/amber tinting
- Volumetric Fog enabled with high scattering intensity
- Light shafts using Volumetric Lightmaps
- Particle systems for floating dust and pollen
- Material parameter collections for time-of-day transitions

**Atmospheric Storytelling:**
The lighting tells the story of an ancient world where time moves differently. The warm, hazy atmosphere suggests a climate warmer than modern Earth, while the quality of light filtering through primitive vegetation creates an otherworldly feeling that reinforces the prehistoric setting.

**Environmental Narrative:**
- Morning mist rising from primordial swamps
- Afternoon heat creating shimmer effects
- Evening fog rolling through valleys
- Night bioluminescence from primitive organisms

**Material Properties for Atmospheric Assets:**
- Moss materials with subsurface scattering for realistic light penetration
- Lichen materials with slight emissive properties
- Rock materials with proper roughness for light interaction
- Vegetation materials with translucency for backlighting effects

**Performance Considerations:**
- LOD system for volumetric effects based on distance
- Dynamic fog density based on weather conditions
- Optimized particle counts for atmospheric effects
- Efficient light shaft rendering using screen-space techniques

This atmospheric system creates the foundation for environmental storytelling, where every ray of light and wisp of fog contributes to the player's immersion in the Cretaceous period.
