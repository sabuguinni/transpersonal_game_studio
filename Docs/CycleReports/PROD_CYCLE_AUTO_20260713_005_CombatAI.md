### 4. Design rationale (Naughty Dog / Griesemer influence)
- **T-Rex (AlwaysEngage, 0% retreat)**: the apex predator never breaks off — creates the "you may not win this" tension the player must route around, not fight.
- **Raptors (CoordinatedFlank)**: designed to read as a "conversation" between three agents reacting to each other's position, not three independent scripts — the ambush should feel premeditated.
- **Triceratops (ChargeIfCornered)**: a defensive combat pattern that punishes the player for cornering a herbivore, reinforcing the survival-realism principle (no gratuitous aggression).
- **Brachiosaurus (FleeOnly)**: explicitly non-combatant — giant herbivores are set dressing/ecology, not encounter fodder, matching the National-Geographic-realism constraint.
