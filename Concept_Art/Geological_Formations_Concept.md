# Geological Formations Concept Art - Cretaceous Period

## Visual Design Brief

### Primary Geological Features
- **Limestone Cliffs**: Towering 800-unit high cliff faces with visible sedimentary layers
- **Volcanic Spires**: Sharp 1200-unit tall volcanic rock formations jutting from the landscape  
- **Sandstone Arches**: Natural arch formations 400 units high, carved by ancient water flow
- **Canyon Walls**: Expansive 600-unit high canyon systems stretching 2000 units wide
- **Mesa Formations**: Flat-topped 300-unit high plateaus with steep sides

### Cave System Architecture
- **Limestone Caves**: Deep multi-chamber systems extending 500 units underground
- **Lava Tubes**: Volcanic cave networks with 4 main chambers, 300 units deep
- **Sandstone Caverns**: Water-carved caves with 6 chambers, 400 units deep
- **Underground Rivers**: Flowing water systems carving through rock, 200 units deep

### Mineral Deposits & Resources
- **Iron Ore Veins**: Red-tinted mineral deposits in cliff faces and cave walls
- **Copper Deposits**: Green-oxidized copper veins running through rock formations
- **Silver Veins**: Metallic silver streaks in deeper cave systems
- **Gold Deposits**: Rare golden mineral veins in protected cave chambers
- **Crystal Formations**: Blue crystalline structures in deepest cave systems

### Underground Water Features
- **Underground Rivers**: Flowing water carving through limestone
- **Aquifer Chambers**: Large underground water reservoirs
- **Hot Springs**: Geothermally heated water sources
- **Mineral Springs**: Water enriched with dissolved minerals

### Geological Hazards
- **Unstable Cliffs**: Areas prone to rockfall and erosion
- **Sinkhole Zones**: Ground collapse areas over limestone caves
- **Volcanic Vents**: Gas emission points from underground volcanic activity
- **Flash Flood Channels**: Dry riverbeds that flood during storms

### Environmental Integration
- Ferns and cycads growing in cave entrances and cliff crevices
- Natural lighting creating dramatic shadows and highlights
- Weathering patterns showing geological age
- Stratified rock layers telling Earth's history
- Integration with existing biome systems (forest, plains, mountains)

### Technical Implementation
- Static mesh actors representing major geological features
- Scalable formations using basic shapes as placeholders
- Grid-based positioning with randomization for natural appearance
- Underground chamber systems connected to surface entrances
- Resource node placement for future crafting/mining systems

This concept serves as the visual foundation for implementing realistic geological diversity in the Cretaceous survival world.
