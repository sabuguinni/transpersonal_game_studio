# Cave Systems Architecture - Underground World Design

## Cave System Cross-Section Analysis

### Multi-Chamber Cave Networks

#### Limestone Cave Systems (Primary Type)
- **Main Entrance**: 3x3x2 unit opening at surface level
- **Entry Chamber**: Large initial chamber for player orientation
- **Connecting Tunnels**: Narrow passages linking chambers (1.5 unit width)
- **Deep Chambers**: 8 interconnected chambers extending to 500 units depth
- **Underground River**: Water flow carving through limestone bedrock

#### Volcanic Lava Tube Networks  
- **Tube Entrance**: Circular opening formed by ancient lava flow
- **Primary Tube**: Main lava tube corridor extending horizontally
- **Branch Chambers**: 4 side chambers formed by lava pooling
- **Depth Range**: 300 units below surface
- **Volcanic Glass**: Obsidian formations for tool crafting

#### Sandstone Cavern Systems
- **Water-Carved Entrance**: Smooth entrance carved by ancient rivers
- **Erosion Chambers**: 6 chambers carved by water flow patterns
- **Sandstone Layers**: Visible geological stratification
- **Depth**: 400 units with natural water drainage
- **Sand Deposits**: Loose sand accumulation in lower chambers

#### Underground River Networks
- **Surface Springs**: Entry points where rivers go underground
- **Carved Channels**: Water-carved passages through bedrock
- **Pool Chambers**: 12 chambers where rivers widen and pool
- **Depth**: 200 units following natural water table
- **Exit Springs**: Where underground rivers resurface

### Mineral Resource Distribution

#### Iron Ore Veins (Red Coloration)
- **Location**: Primarily in limestone and sandstone caves
- **Visibility**: Red streaks and deposits in cave walls
- **Accessibility**: Requires mining tools to extract
- **Quantity**: Common resource for basic tool crafting

#### Copper Deposits (Green Oxidation)
- **Location**: Near water sources and in volcanic caves
- **Appearance**: Green patina and oxidized copper veins
- **Mining Difficulty**: Moderate - requires bronze-age tools
- **Uses**: Advanced tool crafting and decorative items

#### Precious Metal Veins
- **Silver**: Metallic streaks in deeper, protected chambers
- **Gold**: Rare veins in most inaccessible cave systems
- **Crystal Formations**: Blue crystalline structures in deepest chambers
- **Rarity**: Extremely limited quantities for end-game crafting

### Underground Water Features

#### Aquifer Systems
- **Chamber Size**: Large underground reservoirs (500 unit radius)
- **Water Quality**: Fresh water source for survival
- **Access**: Requires cave exploration to discover
- **Depth**: 300 units below surface

#### Geothermal Features
- **Hot Springs**: Heated water from geothermal activity
- **Steam Vents**: Volcanic gas emissions in lava tube systems
- **Mineral Springs**: Water enriched with dissolved minerals
- **Temperature Zones**: Varying heat levels affecting player comfort

### Environmental Hazards

#### Structural Instabilities
- **Rockfall Zones**: Areas where ceiling collapse is possible
- **Unstable Passages**: Tunnels that may collapse under stress
- **Sinkhole Formation**: Surface collapse over large chambers

#### Water Hazards
- **Flash Flooding**: Underground rivers can surge during surface storms
- **Deep Pools**: Risk of drowning in underground water bodies
- **Current Dangers**: Strong underground river currents

#### Atmospheric Hazards
- **Gas Pockets**: Dangerous gas accumulation in sealed chambers
- **Low Oxygen**: Reduced air quality in deep cave systems
- **Toxic Minerals**: Dangerous mineral exposure in certain areas

### Navigation and Exploration

#### Cave Mapping System
- **Landmark Chambers**: Distinctive chambers for navigation reference
- **Tunnel Networks**: Interconnected passages requiring exploration
- **Dead Ends**: Chambers with no further passages
- **Multiple Entrances**: Some cave systems accessible from multiple surface points

#### Lighting Considerations
- **Natural Light**: Limited to entrance areas
- **Torch Requirements**: Player must bring light sources
- **Reflective Surfaces**: Mineral formations that reflect light
- **Shadow Zones**: Areas of complete darkness requiring careful navigation

This architectural framework provides the foundation for implementing realistic, explorable cave systems that enhance survival gameplay through resource gathering, shelter opportunities, and environmental challenges.
