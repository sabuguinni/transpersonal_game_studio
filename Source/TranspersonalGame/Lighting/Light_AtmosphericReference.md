# Cretaceous Period Atmospheric Lighting Reference

## Visual Style Guide

### Primary Lighting Characteristics
- **Sun Color**: Warm golden amber (RGB: 255, 240, 200)
- **Temperature**: 5500K (slightly warmer than modern daylight)
- **Intensity**: 5.0 lux (bright but not harsh)
- **Atmospheric Scattering**: Enhanced blue-orange gradient at horizon

### Volumetric Effects
- **God Rays**: Prominent through forest canopy gaps
- **Atmospheric Perspective**: Misty depth with blue-gray distant mountains
- **Fog Density**: 0.02 (subtle but noticeable)
- **Height Falloff**: 0.2 (concentrated near ground level)

### Color Palette
- **Primary**: Warm amber (#FFB366)
- **Secondary**: Deep forest green (#2D5016)
- **Accent**: Misty blue-gray (#B8C5D1)
- **Shadows**: Cool purple-gray (#4A4458)

### Environmental Mood
The Cretaceous period lighting should evoke:
- Ancient, primordial atmosphere
- Warm but alien sunlight
- Dense, humid air quality
- Mysterious depth in forest areas
- Golden hour feeling throughout day cycle

### Technical Implementation
- Lumen global illumination for realistic bounce lighting
- Volumetric fog for atmospheric depth
- Dynamic sun positioning for day/night cycle
- Temperature-based color shifts
- Scattering intensity for god ray effects

This reference guides all lighting decisions in the prehistoric world environment.
