# Prehistoric Stone Age Dwelling Interior - Concept Documentation

## Visual Concept Description

### Interior Layout
- **Floor Plan**: Single room rectangular space, 6m x 4m
- **Ceiling Height**: 2.5m at peak, sloping to 2m at walls
- **Entrance**: Single doorway facing east for morning light
- **Central Feature**: Stone-lined fire pit with surrounding seating area

### Structural Elements
- **Walls**: Primitive stone construction with wooden support beams
- **Roof Structure**: Wooden framework with thatch or hide covering
- **Floor**: Packed earth with scattered animal hide rugs
- **Support Posts**: Vertical wooden poles reinforcing roof structure

### Interior Furnishings
- **Sleeping Areas**: Raised platforms with animal fur bedding along walls
- **Storage**: Woven baskets and clay vessels in corners
- **Tool Storage**: Wooden pegs and stone shelves for primitive tools
- **Food Preparation**: Flat stone surfaces near fire pit

### Lighting and Atmosphere
- **Primary Light**: Central fire pit with glowing embers
- **Secondary Light**: Filtered daylight through doorway
- **Shadows**: Deep dramatic shadows in corners and under roof structure
- **Atmosphere**: Warm, intimate, protective interior space

### Cultural Artifacts
- **Tools**: Stone axes, spears, and cutting implements hanging on walls
- **Pottery**: Hand-formed clay vessels for water and food storage
- **Textiles**: Woven grass mats and animal hide blankets
- **Decorative Elements**: Simple cave painting-style marks on walls

### Environmental Details
- **Smoke Effects**: Wisps of smoke rising from fire pit
- **Texture Variation**: Rough stone, smooth wood, soft furs
- **Wear Patterns**: Evidence of daily use on floor and seating areas
- **Natural Materials**: All furnishings made from available prehistoric resources

## Gameplay Functionality
- **Shelter Mechanics**: Provides protection from weather and predators
- **Crafting Station**: Fire pit enables tool and food preparation
- **Rest Area**: Sleeping platforms restore player stamina
- **Storage System**: Containers allow item organization and storage

## Archaeological Accuracy
- **Time Period**: Late Paleolithic to early Neolithic transition
- **Construction Methods**: Authentic prehistoric building techniques
- **Material Usage**: Only materials available in Cretaceous period
- **Cultural Context**: Represents early permanent human habitation

This interior design captures the essence of humanity's first permanent dwellings, showing the ingenuity and resourcefulness of our prehistoric ancestors in creating comfortable, functional living spaces with limited technology.
