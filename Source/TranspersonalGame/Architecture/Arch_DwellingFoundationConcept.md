# Cretaceous Period Stone Dwelling Foundation - Concept Documentation

## Visual Concept Description

### Foundation Structure
- **Material**: Weathered limestone blocks arranged in rectangular pattern
- **Construction**: Primitive prehistoric building technique with natural stone fitting
- **Dimensions**: Approximately 6m x 4m foundation footprint
- **Height**: 0.5m raised foundation platform

### Weathering and Age
- **Surface Texture**: Heavily weathered limestone with natural erosion patterns
- **Biological Growth**: Moss and lichen covering 30-40% of stone surfaces
- **Color Palette**: Gray limestone base with green moss accents and brown lichen patches
- **Structural Integrity**: Ancient but stable, showing thousands of years of weathering

### Environmental Integration
- **Surrounding Vegetation**: Prehistoric ferns and cycads growing around edges
- **Ground Interface**: Foundation stones partially embedded in earth
- **Drainage**: Natural gaps between stones for water runoff
- **Orientation**: Aligned with natural terrain contours

### Archaeological Authenticity
- **Construction Method**: Dry stone technique without mortar
- **Tool Marks**: Evidence of primitive stone shaping tools
- **Settlement Pattern**: Foundation suggests small family dwelling
- **Cultural Context**: Early human permanent settlement structure

### Technical Specifications
- **Polygon Count**: 5000-8000 triangles for game optimization
- **Texture Resolution**: 2048x2048 diffuse, normal, and roughness maps
- **LOD Levels**: 3 levels of detail for performance scaling
- **Collision**: Simplified box collision for gameplay interaction

## Gameplay Integration
- **Player Interaction**: Can be used as building foundation for shelter construction
- **Resource Source**: Stones can be harvested for crafting
- **Landmark**: Serves as navigation reference point
- **Historical Significance**: Provides environmental storytelling element

This foundation represents the earliest human attempts at permanent architecture in the Cretaceous period, showing the transition from nomadic to settled lifestyle patterns.
