# Coastal Biome Visual Concept Documentation

## Primary Coastal Environment
**Visual Style**: Rugged prehistoric shoreline with dramatic tidal zones
**Atmosphere**: Salty mist, crashing waves, wind-swept environment
**Water Features**: Massive tidal pools with ancient marine life, rocky tide pools
**Terrain**: Weathered limestone cliffs, volcanic rock formations, sandy beaches

## Key Visual Elements for UE5 Implementation
- **Rock Formations**: Layered sedimentary cliffs, volcanic outcrops, sea stacks
- **Tidal Zones**: Dynamic water levels, exposed reef systems, kelp forests
- **Beach Elements**: Scattered driftwood, shell deposits, ancient coral formations
- **Vegetation**: Hardy coastal plants, salt-resistant ferns, primitive seaweeds
- **Atmospheric Effects**: Ocean spray particles, volumetric fog, dynamic weather

## Marine Ecosystem Features
- **Shallow Waters**: Clear tidal pools with visible marine life
- **Deep Waters**: Darker ocean areas with mysterious depths
- **Reef Systems**: Primitive coral and sponge formations
- **Shoreline Debris**: Massive logs, volcanic rocks, fossilized remains

## Dynamic Elements
- **Tidal System**: Water levels that change over time
- **Wave Action**: Realistic water physics with foam and spray
- **Weather Patterns**: Coastal storms, fog banks, clear skies
- **Wildlife Activity**: Pterosaur flyovers, marine reptile glimpses

## Color Palette
- **Water**: Deep blues, turquoise shallows, white foam
- **Rock**: Gray limestone, black volcanic, rust-colored iron deposits
- **Vegetation**: Muted greens, brown seaweeds, colorful marine plants
- **Sky**: Dramatic clouds, golden sunsets, stormy grays

## Performance Optimization
- **Water LOD**: Multiple detail levels for ocean rendering
- **Particle Culling**: Distance-based spray and mist effects
- **Instanced Rocks**: Efficient rendering of coastal formations
- **Dynamic Loading**: Seamless transition between land and sea biomes

This coastal biome concept provides a diverse environment for survival gameplay, offering unique resources, challenges, and visual variety in our Cretaceous world.
