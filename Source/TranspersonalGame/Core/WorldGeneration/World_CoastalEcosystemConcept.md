# Coastal Ecosystem Concept - Advanced Water Systems

## Visual Concept Description
Prehistoric coastal ecosystem featuring diverse water biomes integrated into the Cretaceous world:

### Primary Coastal Biomes
- **Rocky Cliffs**: Dramatic limestone and basalt cliffs with crashing waves, sea caves, and nesting sites for pterosaurs
- **Sandy Beaches**: Wide tidal zones with shifting dunes, exposed fossil beds, and marine reptile nesting areas  
- **Mangrove Swamps**: Dense root systems creating maze-like waterways, rich biodiversity hotspots
- **Volcanic Coastline**: Active thermal vents, black sand beaches, mineral-rich waters supporting unique ecosystems
- **Coral Reefs**: Shallow water formations with diverse marine life, hunting grounds for aquatic predators

### Marine Life Integration
- **Surface Waters**: Pteranodon and other flying reptiles diving for fish
- **Mid-depth**: Plesiosaurs and marine crocodiles patrolling hunting territories
- **Deep Waters**: Massive mosasaurs and other apex marine predators
- **Tidal Zones**: Ammonites, belemnites, and prehistoric crustaceans in rock pools

### Environmental Features
- **Tidal Mechanics**: Dynamic water levels affecting accessibility and resource availability
- **Underwater Terrain**: Seamounts, abyssal plains, hydrothermal vents creating diverse habitats
- **River Systems**: Major rivers connecting inland biomes to coastal deltas
- **Weather Patterns**: Coastal storms, fog banks, and seasonal variations

### Gameplay Integration
- **Resource Gathering**: Unique coastal materials like salt, shells, and marine fossils
- **Navigation Challenges**: Tidal timing, underwater obstacles, and marine predator territories
- **Ecosystem Interactions**: Food chain dynamics between terrestrial and marine environments
- **Exploration Opportunities**: Hidden sea caves, underwater ruins, and isolated islands

This concept supports the realistic survival gameplay while showcasing the diverse aquatic ecosystems of the Cretaceous period.
