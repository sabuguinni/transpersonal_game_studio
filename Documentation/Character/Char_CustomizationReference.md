# Cretaceous Period Character Customization Reference

## Overview
Visual reference for tribal human character variants in the Cretaceous survival game. All designs focus on scientific accuracy and realistic survival adaptations.

## Character Variants

### Male Survivors
- **Young Hunter** (Age 18-25)
  - Lean, athletic build
  - Minimal scars, fresh tattoos
  - Simple hide loincloth, bone necklace
  - Stone spear, flint knife
  - Skin tone: Light tan from sun exposure

- **Experienced Warrior** (Age 30-40)
  - Muscular, battle-hardened build
  - Multiple scars from dinosaur encounters
  - Elaborate hide armor, tooth jewelry
  - Stone axe, bone club, hide shield
  - Skin tone: Deep bronze, weathered

- **Elder Shaman** (Age 50+)
  - Wiry, weathered build
  - Ritual scars, extensive tattoos
  - Feathered cloak, bone staff
  - Medicine pouch, carved totems
  - Skin tone: Dark, deeply lined

### Female Survivors
- **Young Gatherer** (Age 18-25)
  - Agile, compact build
  - Decorative face paint, minimal scars
  - Woven grass skirt, shell jewelry
  - Gathering basket, digging stick
  - Skin tone: Medium tan

- **Experienced Crafter** (Age 30-40)
  - Strong arms, calloused hands
  - Work-related scars, practical tattoos
  - Leather apron, tool belt
  - Stone hammer, bone awl, hide scraper
  - Skin tone: Olive, sun-darkened

- **Elder Matriarch** (Age 50+)
  - Dignified bearing, wise eyes
  - Ceremonial scars, status tattoos
  - Elaborate fur cloak, precious stones
  - Carved staff, ritual implements
  - Skin tone: Deep brown, age-spotted

## Clothing Materials

### Animal Hides
- **Dinosaur Leather**: Thick, durable, dark brown
- **Small Mammal Fur**: Soft, warm, various colors
- **Reptile Scales**: Flexible, waterproof, iridescent
- **Bird Feathers**: Decorative, colorful, status symbols

### Crafted Items
- **Woven Grass**: Lightweight, breathable, tan/green
- **Bark Cloth**: Rough texture, brown/gray
- **Sinew Cord**: Strong, flexible, off-white
- **Plant Dyes**: Natural colors for decoration

## Jewelry and Accessories

### Bone Items
- **Dinosaur Teeth**: Large predator fangs as pendants
- **Small Bones**: Finger bones, ribs as beads
- **Carved Bone**: Intricate patterns, clan symbols
- **Bone Needles**: Functional and decorative

### Stone Elements
- **Polished Stones**: Smooth river rocks, colorful
- **Carved Totems**: Animal shapes, spiritual symbols
- **Precious Minerals**: Rare finds, high status
- **Flint Chips**: Sharp, functional, gray/black

### Natural Materials
- **Shells**: From ancient seas, spiral patterns
- **Seeds**: Large nuts, dried berries
- **Claws**: Predator talons, hunting trophies
- **Scales**: Iridescent fish/reptile scales

## Facial Decorations

### Paint Patterns
- **War Paint**: Bold stripes, intimidating designs
- **Ceremonial**: Dots, spirals, clan markings
- **Camouflage**: Earth tones, hunting patterns
- **Status**: Complex designs showing rank

### Natural Pigments
- **Red Ochre**: Iron oxide, earth red
- **Charcoal**: Deep black, soot-based
- **Clay**: White/gray, kaolin-based
- **Plant Dyes**: Green, yellow, purple from vegetation

## Weathering and Age Effects

### Skin Weathering (0.0 to 1.0 scale)
- **0.0-0.2**: Young, smooth skin, minimal exposure
- **0.3-0.5**: Adult, some sun damage, work calluses
- **0.6-0.8**: Mature, weathered, multiple scars
- **0.9-1.0**: Elder, deeply lined, age spots

### Scar Types
- **Claw Marks**: Parallel lines from predator attacks
- **Bite Wounds**: Circular/oval from dinosaur teeth
- **Tool Cuts**: Clean lines from crafting accidents
- **Burn Marks**: Irregular patches from fire

### Tattoo Styles
- **Geometric**: Simple lines, dots, basic shapes
- **Animal**: Stylized dinosaurs, mammals, birds
- **Tribal**: Clan symbols, status indicators
- **Spiritual**: Abstract patterns, ritual significance

## Implementation Notes
- All variants use the same base mesh with material variations
- Weathering level affects normal maps and specular values
- Gender differences are subtle, focused on practical adaptations
- Color palettes emphasize earth tones and natural materials
- Avoid fantasy elements - maintain prehistoric realism
